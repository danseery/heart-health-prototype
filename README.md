# HeartHealth AI Prototype

HeartHealth AI is a prototype for a cardiologist-reviewed heart health platform that helps people understand their cardiac risk scores, learn what their numbers mean, and find trustworthy next steps in plain English.

The concept centers on a free heart health risk assessment that uses established clinical calculators, then converts the results into an accessible AI-generated educational report grounded in content reviewed by Dr. Brian Chen, DO.

## Goals

- Help users complete a heart health risk assessment in about five minutes.
- Calculate and explain risk signals such as ASCVD 10-year risk, Framingham risk, CHA2DS2-VASc inputs, heart age, blood pressure, cholesterol, and lifestyle factors.
- Present results in a dashboard that is easy to scan, color-coded, and suitable to save or share with a doctor.
- Provide plain-English AI summaries that explain risk factors without replacing medical advice.
- Build a subscription content library with cardiologist-authored videos and guides on topics such as AFib, cholesterol, hypertension, test results, heart failure, and prevention.
- Offer a RAG-powered AI health coach that answers subscriber questions using curated source material and cites the underlying content.
- Support a simple monetization model with a free tier, a premium monthly plan, and a one-time detailed report.

## Prototype Scope

The initial mockups cover six core experiences:

1. Landing page and acquisition funnel
2. Multi-step risk assessment questionnaire
3. Results dashboard with AI educational summary
4. Subscriber content library
5. AI health coach chat
6. Pricing page with Stripe-powered checkout

## Product Principles

- Trust first: emphasize cardiologist-reviewed content, clear attribution, and medical disclaimers.
- Plain language: explain clinical terms and scores in a way non-clinicians can understand.
- Low friction: allow users to start free without an account and handle missing health values gracefully.
- Source-grounded AI: responses should cite the curated content used to generate them.
- Educational only: the product should support informed conversations with clinicians, not diagnose or replace medical care.
